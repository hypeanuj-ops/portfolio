About me

Curious by nature, building by habit.

I am a Mechanical Engineering student at UMBC, driven by a fascination for how physical systems and smart design solve real-world challenges. Whether it's analyzing 3D assemblies in CAD or understanding the thermodynamics of an engine, I am committed to building solutions that are both efficient and reliable.

My journey began in Nepal, and moving to the U.S. during my junior year of high school taught me how to adapt quickly to new environments—a skill I bring to every engineering project.

Outside of the classroom, you can find me working on my car or practicing guitar. I believe that the best engineers aren't just good at math; they bring a broad curiosity from their hobbies to solve narrow, complex technical problems.

This portfolio is a living document of my growth from a student to a professional engineer. Check back often to see my latest designs.
